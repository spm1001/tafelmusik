Bons capture what (--what) and why (--why) but not how — the choreography between actions. This gap became visible when calute Phase 2 required four coordinated actions with a dangerous partial state: swapping the observer before drift tracking is ready makes Claude completely deaf to major surgery. The --what fields on individual actions are self-contained, but the sequencing constraint lives *between* them, nowhere in the bon model. The workaround is an implementation brief (docs/calute-phase2-brief.md) referenced from the outcome's --what. This works but it's convention, not structure. Filed bon-tufihu in the bon repo for a --how mechanism. The broader lesson: any multi-action outcome with ordering dependencies needs choreography documentation, and future /close sessions should check for this gap before filing bons without it.
