Selection and commenting are two distinct intents that share the same gesture (highlighting text). The original design auto-focused the compose card on any selection, which made it impossible to select text for editing (delete, replace, format). After four iterations (auto-focus, escape-to-edit, no-focus, revert), the resolution is: compose card appears on selection as a visual hint, but focus stays in the editor. Click the compose card to write a comment. The asymmetry is a browser primitive — programmatic selection works (compose→editor) but a click always sets the cursor (editor→compose). Escape is the standard pattern for dismissing panels with selection preserved, but felt wrong to the user. The "no-auto-focus" approach won because it doesn't fight the browser.
